City Information:

My case study city is Taipei. Taipei is the capital city of Taiwan, a small island 100 miles off the coast of southeastern China. Taipei was established in the early 18th century and is currently the top manufacturer of semiconductors. Taipei is really small compared to other cities, for example, Singpaore has a land area of about 729km², while Taipei only has 272km², making it close to 1/3 of the size. Taipei has a population of 2.6 million, and a density of about 659 people per km².

What is urbanisation? Urbanisation is a change from people living and working in the countryside to cities. Urbanisation is a bigger proportion of people living in urban areas than in rural areas.


What is urban growth? Urban growth is the spreading of urban buildings like high-rise buildings into country-side/rural areas. In Taiwan, urban growth is visible in the way that you can see green patches slowly disappearing and turning gray on Google timelapse.


What is the aim of this presentation? My aim and focus of this presentation is to provide details about the opportunities Taipei has, and the challenges that it faces. My opportunity is how the economy of Taipei has grown through urban growth. My challenge is the environmental problems in Taipei, what are its causes, and what are some potential solutions for this challenge.
